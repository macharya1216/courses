Economic Philosophy

Two Perspectives on Economics: Scarcity vs. Abundance

Economics can be viewed through two fundamentally different lenses that shape how we approach resource allocation, decision-making, and human welfare. The traditional scarcity-focused view emphasizes competition and zero-sum thinking, while the abundance-focused view emphasizes optimization and collective well-being.

Understanding these perspectives is crucial because they influence policy decisions, business strategies, and personal financial choices. The lens we choose shapes not just what we study in economics, but how we live our lives and structure our societies.

Contrasting Perspectives

ü•É Glass Empty View (Scarcity Mindset)

Core Belief: Resources are fundamentally limited, and people must compete to secure their "fair share." This creates a zero-sum environment where one person's gain is necessarily another's loss.

Focus: Competition, hoarding, protecting what you have, fighting for limited resources.

Example: "There aren't enough good jobs for everyone, so I need to work 80 hours a week to stay competitive and keep others from taking my position."

üå± Glass Full View (Abundance Mindset)

Core Belief: The central question is how to optimally allocate existing resources to maximize collective happiness and individual fulfillment.

Focus: Optimization, collaboration, creating value, making better decisions for better outcomes.

Example: "How can we redesign work structures so that everyone can contribute their unique talents while maintaining work-life balance and community well-being?"

Healthcare Policy:
